Cassava Cake

With a thick and moist layer of cake made of fresh cassava root, sweetened condensed milk & evaporated milk, and rich coconut cream–cassava cake is a Filipino party decadence, especially with its extra silky custard topping! Cassava cake is another type of dessert that incorporates a root vegetable. It’s also known in other names like yuca, manioca, and aipim across the world. It was introduced via colonization by Spain in the 16th century. Cassava cake is a type of traditional rice baked cake (bibingka), which uses cassava root as its base instead of glutinous rice.

Prep Time

15 min.

Cook Time

1 hrs

Servings

6 people

instructions

step 1

Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C). Grease a 9x13 inch baking dish with butter or cooking spray.

step 2

In a large bowl, mix together the grated cassava, coconut milk, evaporated milk, condensed milk, sugar, eggs, melted butter, grated cheese (if using), vanilla extract (if using), and salt. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

step 3

Pour the mixture into the prepared baking dish.

step 4

Bake for about 1 hour or until the top is golden brown and the cake is set. You can test if the cake is done by inserting a toothpick into the center of the cake. If it comes out clean, the cake is done.

step 5

Remove the cake from the oven and let it cool before slicing and serving.

step 6

Optional: If you want to add a little more color to the top of the cake, you can broil it for a minute or two, but be careful not to let it burn.

step 7

Serve and enjoy! The cassava cake can be served warm or cold and makes a great dessert or snack.

ingredients

2 cups grated cassava (fresh or frozen)

1 can coconut milk (13.5 to 14 ounces)

1 can evaporated milk (12 ounces)

1 can condensed milk (14 ounces)

1 cup sugar (or to taste)

3 large eggs

1/2 cup butter (melted)

1/2 cup grated cheese (optional)

1 cup powdered sugar

1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)
